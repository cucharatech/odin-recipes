Ají de Gallina

Description

This is one of the best reviewed peruvian dishes. It is a fusion between catalan (spanish) and peruvian cuisine. It has its origins in the XIV century as a dessert in Spain. Once it came to the spanish colonys (or the territory now known as Peru) it changed and became a main dish.

This recipe's difficult is moderated. You can have it done in less than 1 hour.

Ingredients

  • 1 henn breast (or chicken)
  • 4 yellow potatoes
  • 4 slices of whitebread
  • 3/4 cup evaporated milk
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 4 Yellow aji pepper (without stems and seeds)
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic paste
  • 1 red onion
  • Vegetable oil
  • 4 boiled eggs

Preparation

  1. Cook the yellow potatoes in salted water until tender when pierced with a fork. Then let them cool and slice them into quarters
  2. Let the bread soak in the milk.
  3. Boil the breast with some salt in a pot with water (it must cover the breast). Let it cook for 10- 15 minutes.
  4. Reserve the stock and the breast.
  5. In a large pan, bring it to heat and add some oil. Cook the onions until tender. Then add the garlic paste and cook for five minutes.
  6. Process the yellow peppers with some oil. Then add it to the pan and cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Add the soak bread and stir until it dissolves. Add chicken stock until you get your desire texture.
  8. Shred the cooled breast into bite-sized pieces, then add it to the pan and stir
  9. Serve it on top of the potatoes and add a 1 boiled egg in halves on top of it
  10. You can enjoy it with a side of rice.

Enjoy!
